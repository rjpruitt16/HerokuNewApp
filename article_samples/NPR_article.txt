Kentucky Governor Apologizes For Comments On Teachers' StrikeEnlarge this image toggle caption Bryan Woolston/AP Bryan Woolston/APKentucky Gov. Matt Bevin is apologizing for saying a teachers' strike left hundreds of thousands of children vulnerable to sexual assault and drug use. Bevin's comments provoked anger and a rapid backlash from teachers' groups and state politicians. Matt Bevin released a video in which he apologized for comments he made on teachers' protests. The protests in Kentucky follow teachers' demonstrations across several states, demanding higher pay and more funding for education.
